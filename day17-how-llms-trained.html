A model is built in three stages. Ask the same thing — "How do I make tea?" — and step through them. After ① Pretraining it just autocompletes text. After ② SFT it follows the instruction. After ③ RLHF it's genuinely helpful, well-formatted, and refuses unsafe requests. Same question, three very different answers.

A raw or instruction-only model would often just answer. After RLHF, the model learned humans prefer a polite refusal — so it declines.

A raw model only predicts the next token; instruction tuning teaches it to follow requests; RLHF aligns it to human preferences (helpful, honest, harmless). This demo uses hand-written answers so each stage is deterministic; real models learn these behaviours from huge corpora, curated instruction sets, and millions of human preference judgements.
