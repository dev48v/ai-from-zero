🧩 Fine-tuning vs RAG — Two Ways to Teach a Model

Got a job for an LLM? It tells you which approach fits.

RAG looks things up at question time; fine-tuning bakes new behaviour into the weights by retraining. Pick a scenario and see which wins — and why.

Recommended approach
—
📚 RAG — give it notes
Store your data externally; fetch relevant bits per question and put them in the prompt. Best for FACTS that change.
🏋️ Fine-tune — retrain it
Continue training on examples so new STYLE/SKILL is baked into the weights. Best for BEHAVIOUR/format.

🔨 The decision, in practice

    
Most real products use a base model + RAG first, and fine-tune only if needed.

    
    
      
  1. 1
    Start with prompting + RAG
    Cheapest, fastest, no training. Covers most "answer from my data" needs and keeps facts current. Try this before anything heavier.
    2. 
      
  2. 2
    Fine-tune for consistent behaviour
    When you need a reliable tone, format, or a narrow skill (always output this JSON, always answer in this brand voice), train on examples of the behaviour.
    3. 
      
  3. 3
    They combine
    Fine-tune for HOW it answers + RAG for WHAT facts it uses. A support bot can be fine-tuned to sound on-brand AND RAG over the live help-centre.
    4. 
      
  4. 4
    Watch the failure modes
    Fine-tuning can't keep up with changing facts (retrain to update) and can make the model forget other things. RAG depends on good retrieval — bad chunks → bad answers.
