🌡️ Temperature & Sampling — the Creativity Dial

One number decides "safe & repetitive" vs "creative & risky"

The model gives probabilities for the next word. Temperature reshapes them before it picks. Low → it grabs the top word every time. High → the odds flatten and rarer words get a real chance.

Prompt: "The weather today is ___"
Temperature 0.8

↑ Drag toward 0: bars spike on "sunny", samples all say "sunny" (deterministic). Drag high: bars even out, samples scatter across "stormy", "unpredictable", even odd ones. T ≈ 0.7–1.0 is the usual creative range.

🔨 The sampling knobs

    
Three settings control how the next token is chosen.

    
    
      
  1. 1
    temperature
    Reshapes the probabilities: p ∝ p^(1/T). T→0 sharpens to the top word (deterministic); T→1 keeps them as-is; T>1 flattens (wilder). Factual task → low; brainstorming → higher.
    2. 
      
  2. 2
    top-k
    Only ever sample from the k most-likely words; zero out the rest. Stops the model from occasionally picking something absurd from the long tail.
    3. 
      
  3. 3
    top-p (nucleus)
    Keep the smallest set of words whose probabilities sum to p (e.g. 0.9), sample from those. Adapts: few options when the model is sure, many when it isn't.
    4. 
      
  4. 4
    Rule of thumb
    Code, extraction, factual Q&A → temperature ≈ 0 (reproducible). Marketing copy, ideas, fiction → 0.7–1.0. Set temperature OR top-p, not both aggressively.
